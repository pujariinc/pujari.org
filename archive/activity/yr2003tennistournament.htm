Rules

  1. All players are required to report at Kingsgate Subdivision Tennis Court 15 minutes before their first match – no exceptions. Please look for Kallol Nandi once you get there. Before the tournament, if you have any questions, please call Bhaskar Banerjee (678-362-6310) or Neel Majumdar (678-517-6293).
  2. If you haven’t already paid the tournament entry fee of $12/team, please pay in cash before your first match.
  3. The format for the tournament will be as follows: The first Round will consist of a round robin match within each of the four groups. Every team may not play every other team in the groups having 4 teams. Only one team will qualify from each group for the next Round (II) – this will be the semifinal round. Round III will be the final. All Round I matches are one set matches. Rounds II and III will be best of three sets matches.
  4. One team from each group will qualify for Round II. If there is a tie within a group, game scores for Round I will be taken into account for qualifying to Round II.
  5. We will provide drinks during matches but please also bring additional drinks and snacks according to your requirements.
  6. Tennis balls will be provided. Tennis balls will be replaced after every 2 sets.
  7. The tournament will generally follow the USTA tennis rules and code of conduct. It may be a good idea to peruse these rules before the tournament – you can find them at http://www.usta.com/rules/default.sps?iType=923.
  8. All disputes will be ruled by the tournament Director, whose decision will be binding.
  9. By entering this tournament, you are accepting all liabilities regarding personal injuries during the match, irrespective of the cause.

Pujari Group Organizes its Inaugural One-Day Doubles Tennis Tournament 

Pujari Group, the premier non-profit organization representing the Bengali community in Georgia, organized its first-ever one-day doubles tennis tournament on Saturday, May 31st, 2003. The venue was the tennis courts at the Kingsgate subdivision in Woodstock. The championship was a grand success with 16 doubles teams or 32 players participating. The mood was festive with quite a few friends and relatives of the players watching and cheering the players. The organizers arranged for the venue, provided soft drinks, and match balls for the players. Cobb Mortgage was the sponsor for the tournament.

The tournament consisted of three rounds: Round I, Semi-Finals and the Final. In the interest of time and practicality, Round I matches were all best of one set matches. However, the Semi-final matches and the Final were best of three sets.

In Round I, the 16 teams were divided into 4 groups, each comprised of 4 teams. The top team from each of the groups made it to the semifinals.

The group winners are as follows:
Group A: Subhojit Roy & Mukesh Achtani
Group B: Swapan Mondol & Srinivas Yamujala
Group C: Rajeev Kumar & Raghu Pani
Group D: Soumen Ghosh & Joydeb Majumdar

The top teams from Group C and Group B duked it out in the first semi-final. Soumen Ghosh & Joydeb Majumdar fought hard but lost the first set 3-6 to Rajeev Kumar & Raghu Pani. However, the second set was totally one-sided with Soumen & Joydeb getting overwhelmed by the superior play of Rajeev and Raghu, losing 0-6.

The second semi-final was much more competitive. The fortunes of the match swung from one side to the other as each team gave its best in trying to reach the final. The Group A winners, Subhojit Roy and Mukesh Achtani, looked like the team in control when they won the first set 6-3. However, the Group B winners, Swapan Mondol & Srinivas Yamujala, came back strong with an easy 6-0 win in the second set. The third set saw both teams give their best, with Swapan and Srinivas wrapping it up 6-3.

In the much-anticipated final, Rajeev and Raghu were merciless as they drubbed Swapan and Srinivas 6-1, 6-0. Although the losing team played a fairly good brand of tennis, Rajeev and Raghu were clearly a cut above the rest. Raghu’s blistering serves and Rajeev’s sense of positioning were a treat to watch.

Mr. Gauranga Banik of Cobb Mortgage presented the trophies to the winners: Rajeev Kumar and Raghu Pani.

All in all, it was a fun day for the South Asian community, giving a good excuse to soak in the glorious weather, interact with friends, and last, but not least, enjoy the magnificent sport of tennis.

The organizers extend special thanks to Kallol Nandi, the coordinator, whose organizational skills were instrumental to the success of the tournament. Special thanks also go out to Mr. Anjan Datta Gupta, Ms. Sheri Creslein, Mr. Sudipto Ghosh, Mr. Soumen Ghosh and Mr. Shubhojit Roy for their assistance in organizing the tournament. Last, but not least, the tournament would have never happened without the persistence shown by Mr. Bhaskar Banerjee, who made this concept a reality.
