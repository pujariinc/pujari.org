Durga Puja 2009 organized by Pujari

  • Sept. 25th, 2009 (Fri), Sept. 26th, 2009 (Sat) & Sept. 27th, 2009 (Sun)
    at Berkmar High School,

Pujari will celebrate its 23rd Durga Puja on 25th, 26th and 27th September, 2009 followed by Anjali, Prasad, delicious food and wonderful cultural programs. Come join us in worshipping Maa Durga in a traditional Bengali way and enjoy three days of unlimited fun, sumptuous authentic Bengali food and snacks, unparalleled cultural programs and the biggest of all, tons of "nirbhejal bangali adda".
This year promises to be special with THREE noted guest artists of International fame – Alak Roychoudhury on 25th; Santoneel Dhar on 26th and 'Elvis of the East' Alamgir will be with us on Sunday, 27th. Besides that we will have kids' productions, youth hour, pottery workshop, Live orchestra with local talented musicians, dancers, singers, and much more...

Pottery Workshop
The event is a free event to youth which will focus on the awareness of a nearly extinct tradition of India through a hand on workshop. Inhabitants of the Indian subcontinent have known the art of modeling and painting since prehistoric times. It is evident that pottery culture, mural or painting is a very old tradition in India. The country has a rich tradition of clay crafts and pottery throughout the country. There is hardly any festival or ritual, which is complete without the use of earthen lamps, the earliest example of pottery culture. The young generation will learn the rich art and culture that was once predominant in Indian subcontinent, the influence of this art in their daily life and how did it help them to develop the ancient civilization. They will experience the uniqueness of this industry by actively participating in the workshop. They will get exposure to the importance of an industry which is eco-friendly, absolutely pollution free and provides ample opportunity to grow in the long term with a sustainable plan to make it economically competitive. The workshop will be conducted by experienced teacher Tumpa Bhattacharyya, a graduate from Kala Bhavana Santiniketan in the field of Ceramic/Pottery and Sutapa Datta, a leading entrepreneur in the areas of graphic designing and digital art. Materials and other equipment will be supplied by Pujari and there is no cost involved to the participants. Please register by emailing to sutapadatta@gmail.com or tumpa.bhatta@gmail.com. Early registration is appreciated due to limitation in available space.


Konya Se Ononya
Konya Se Ononya is a mega production involving more than 40 local talents. It is a medley of live songs, dances, narration and recitation anchored by live orchestra. The theme portrays "NARI" and her different incarnations as she progresses through life. Few of the talented singers participating are Debasri Dutta, Taslima Sultana (Polly), Haimanti Mukhopadhyay, Tareq, Prabir Bhattacharya, Santanu Kar, Saibal Sengupta, Rahul Ray, Anindo De etc. belting out popular and nostalgic songs like Jibone Ki Pabona, Bela Bose, Boli o Nanadi, Kono ek Gayer Bodhu, Brittakare Ghuri (Bhoomi), Shei Raate Raat Chilo Purnima, Modhur amar mayer hashi, She amar chhoto bon, Amar swapne dekha rajkonna and more. The dances will be choreographed and performed by some of our immensely talented choreographers and dancers like Banhi Nandi, Indrani Kar, Minnie Homchowdhury, Jaba Chaudhuri, Jaba Ghosh, Sonia Nandi, Suporna Bagchi, Baishakhi Mukherjee, Suporna Chaudhuri etc. The whole program will be knit around a superlative script, narration and recitation by our voice talents Richa Sarkar, Sutapa Das, Sanjib Datta, Biswanath Bhattacharya and Subhashree Nandy. Last but not the least is the orchestra team led by Amitava Sen (keyboards), Tareq (guitar), Byasdeb Saha (tabla/djembe) and Prabir Nandi (triple congo). Backdrop screen designed by Sutapa Datta and directed by Subhasree Nandy, this will be a program of the highest quality that you definitely would not want to miss.
