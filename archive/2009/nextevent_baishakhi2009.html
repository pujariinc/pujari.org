Baishakhi 2009 organized by Pujari

  • April 11th, 2009 at Berkmar High School
We are pleased to announce that we will be celebrating Baishakhi (Bengali New Year Celebrations) on 11th April 2009 at Berkmar High School. Please mark your calendar. You, your family and friends are cordially invited to grace this festival.
Event starts around 3:00 PM and also have a variety of attractions lined up including dinner. Please view the program details in the Anushthan Suchi.

Special Attraction: Bengali songs by Aparna Chakravarty




Aparna Chakravarty

Aparna is a second generation Indian of Bengali descent who grew up in the New York / New Jersey area. As a teenager, Aparna was trained in North Indian Hindustani vocal music in the Patiala Gharana under teacher Manjit Kaur in New York . Her teacher is a disciple of Ustad Munawar Ali Khan, son of Ustad Bade Gulam Ali Khan. As a child she was also trained in Raag Pradhan, Nazrul Geeti, Bhajans, Rabindra Sangeet and several other types of Bengali music in New Jersey . Her repertoire includes Bengali Modern/Adhunik music, Hindi Film songs and Western songs.

Musical Background

In 2007, Aparna was selected in the top 22 at the A R RAHMAN talent hunt out of close to 500 contestants. She was chosen to audition in front of Mr. Rahman in NY in 2007. Aparna has performed with Bollywood singer Abhijeet Bhattacharya (voice of Shah Rukh Khan) and did two major shows with Abhijeet in Cleveland, Ohio and Chicago, Illinois. Aparna has also performed at the Sahara Sangeet Music Awards 2006, in San Francisco, in front of Bollywood music directors such as Pritam and Shankar-Ehsaan-Loy. Aparna has performed all over the United States in prestigious functions such as the North American Bengali Conferences (NABC), American Association of Physicians of Indian Origin(AAPI) and various Durga Puja and fundraising events.

Personal

Aside from vocal music, Aparna is trained in the Bharathanathyam, a form of South Indian Classical dance. Academically, Aparna has a Bachelors and a Masters degree in Electrical Engineering from The Cooper Union School of Engineering, New York and The University of Illinois respectively. She has worked for major companies such as AT&T Bell Laboratories, Lucent and Motorola. Aparna is married and lives with her husband and three children in Jacksonville, Florida.
