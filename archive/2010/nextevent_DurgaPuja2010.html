Durga Puja 2010 organized by Pujari

  • October 15th, 2010 7:30 pm (Friday)
    at Berkmar High School,
    405 Pleasant Hill Rd. NW, Lilburn, GA 30047

Pujari will celebrate its 24th Durga Puja on 15th, 16th and 17th October, 2010 followed by Anjali, prasad, delicious food and wonderful cultural programs.

Come join us in worshipping Maa Durga in a traditional Bengali way and enjoy three days of unlimited fun, sumptuous authentic Bengali food and snacks, unparalleled cultural programs and the biggest of all, tons of "nirbhejal bangali adda".

This year promises to be special with THREE noted guest artists of International fame from Calcutta – Somlata, Nirmalya and Parnava.

Besides that we will have kids' productions, youth hour, Live orchestra with local talented musicians, dancers, singers, and much more...

On the decorations side, this year the puja decoration theme is 'Dakshini' - meaning "hailing from the south".

Our Theme: ‘Dakshini’ portrays the essence of our decoration in South Indian style.
Presentation: Our vibrant color-scheme draws from the multiple colors of 'Kathakali' costumes. Our 'Mukuts', motifs and 'Vahanas' are inspired by the Mysore and Tanjore school of art with slight variations.
Team members: Anupa, Aradhana, Sharmila, Sharbari, Paromita, Tumpa, Sonali, Rupak, Abhijeet, Sonia, Nandini, Sumona, Pranesh, Kajal, Surajit, Sharmistha, Anusuya, Sanjukta, Jasmine, Akash, Remi, Paroma, Esha, Suraaj, Rimjhim, Brishti & Tania. View the Decoration Team collage.

Why celebrate Durga Puja with Pujari? See some of things that differentiates us from others:
  • Sculpture of Ma Durga, Durga Puja rituals and eye-catching decoration
  • Three days of multi-generation cultural shows
  • Three international artists from Calcutta on 3 days - Somlata, Nirmalya and Parnava
  • Buffet-style, sumptuous food for all 3 days
  • Warm hospitality
  • Convenient location
  • Enough indoor space for kids to play with lots of kids activities
  • Ample space for "Adda" and relaxing atmosphere
  • "Best Couple" prize during Durga Puja
  • "Dhunuchi Nach" competition
  • Hands on art and designing experience
  • Professional auditorium with awesome acoustics
  • Subscription/donations are tax deductable
  • Special discount for Diwali celebration (If you attend Durga puja for 3 days)

Artist Showcase


Somlata Acharyya Chowdhury

Somlata Acharyya Chowdhury, daughter of Shyamol Acharyya Chowdhury and Uma Acharyya Chowdhury was classically trained under the tutelage of Pt. Biresh Roy at the tender age of nine. She continued her quest for learning North India Classical Music for over fifteen years since. She is affluent with most forms and genres of music today.

Whether Classical, Western, Pop or Rabindrasangeet, she has performed in diverse genres of music. Somlata has now carved quite a niche for herself in the music fraternity, specially for her classical and western performances.

You can find more about Somlata at her official website at www.somlata.com.




Nirmalya Roy

Nirmalya is trained in classical Hindustani music and is on an exciting journey to bring Raag based songs to the public venues all over the U.S. and India.

He is currently working with the ever popular Bengali Zee TV SaReGaMaPa program as a music director, bringing the program to great heights and viewing pleasure in Bengal. . He has risen to heights of composing music for Bengali serials - "Kanta Tarer Bera", "Ek Atasher Niche", and "Bouchuri Pratibindo". He has recently recorded on Bikram Ghosh's lastes album to be released soon.

Last year he was part of Pandit Ravi Shankar’s 2009 Hollywood Bowl Program in Los Angeles, CA. He enthralled the audience at the NABC Sammelan, 2010 in Atlantic City with his style and presentation.

His background and bio is available at www.nirmalyaroy.com. He is currently involved in teaching students through Rhythm and Raag-An Institute for Indian Performing Arts. He serves as the education advisor in the Institute. He is part of the Center for World Music Faculty in their outreach program in the University of Illinois at Champaign-Urbana.


Parnava Banerjee

Parnava was born in Kolkata in 1987. His parents reminisce that he could sing before he could talk. So they spared no pains in fulfilling the great potential the child showed.

On 30th July, 2006 the memorable time came before Parnava when he stood champion & was honored by Golden Voice Award from Zee TV Bangla.

His first album "Swapno Kuri" was released on Mahalaya (October 10th, 2007) from Prime Music. In 2008, his second new Bengali Album "Na Bala Katha" was released on the eve of Durga Puja from Prime Music and his 3rd Bengali Album "Roddur Hotey Cheya" in the year 2009 was very popular among the music lovers.

At the age of five Parnava started to learn songs under the guidance of his aunt, Smt. Arundhuti Basu Roy. When aged ten, he was admitted in the Musical Institution "Shrutinandan", led by Pt. Ajay Chakraborty. As a student of Rahara Ramkrishna Mission Boys Home, he used to participate in different musical competitions & achieved regular success. As his musical genius prompts him to respect and cultivate all kinds of good songs, he has been proving his unique excellence in various spheres of music. Parnava is very much interested in varieties of songs starting from Bengali Raag Sangeet to Modern Bengali Songs, Ghazals & Hindi Songs.


Subrata Bhattacharya

Subrata is on deck to provide his phenomenal support to the evening's selections. His accomplishments and bio are available at www.subratabhattacharya.net. His latest milestone is his CD release last spring called Naad-The Everlasting Sound. On this album you will also hear Nirmalya’s voice.

In addition, Subrata is a co- founder of Rhythm and Raag-An Institute for Indian Performing Arts. He is teaching and is an artistic advisor in this organization. He is with the UIUC Center for World Music faculty and is involved with their world music outreach work in Urbana-Champaign schools.
